# P2D: ADHD Productivity App Landscape

> **Scope**: Comprehensive catalogue of 54 ADHD-specific and ADHD-marketed productivity apps as of Feb 2026. Includes funding data, platform positioning, and gap analysis against functional problems. Evidence tier: Tier 2–3 (market research, public funding data).

> **Analysis**: For market gap scoring implications, see Analysis/S6_Market_Gap_Assessment.md.

## Methodology Note

Comprehensive sweep of ADHD-specific and ADHD-marketed productivity apps conducted 2026-02-06. Research sources included: roundup articles (ADDitude Magazine, AuDHD Psychiatry, Saner.AI, Fluidwave, Morgen, AFFiNE), directories (ToolFinder, Neurodiversity Directory, Apps for ADHD), community forums (Reddit r/ADHD, Hacker News), Product Hunt launches, Crunchbase/Tracxn/PitchBook funding data, TechCrunch coverage, and individual app websites. Scope is limited to **productivity tools** (task management, focus, scheduling, body doubling, voice capture, meeting notes, project management). Therapeutic/clinical apps (CBT-based symptom management like Inflow, medication trackers like Medisafe) and pure coaching services (Shimmer) are noted but not deep-dived.

### Key Observation

The ADHD productivity app space is dramatically bifurcated:
- **General tools adopted by ADHD users** attract massive funding (Motion: $75M, Endel: $22M, Reclaim: $9.5M)
- **Purpose-built ADHD productivity tools** remain severely underfunded. The most-funded ADHD-specific tool is Tiimo at $5.99M. No ADHD-specific productivity app has raised a Series A.
- **Body doubling** is the best-funded ADHD-specific category, with FLOWN ($5.13M) and Flow Club ($5M) leading.

### Positioning Insights

1. **Single-problem ADHD specialists** are almost all indie/bootstrapped. None has raised >$1M or achieved >50K paying users. The tools are beloved but tiny.

2. **Multi-problem ADHD platforms** are the emerging competitive battleground. Tiimo leads with Apple App of Year 2025 and $6M raised, but has not yet reached Series A. Lunatask, Saner.AI, and Amazing Marvin compete here but all remain small.

3. **General tools marketed to ADHD** capture most of the funding. Motion ($75M), Reclaim ($9.5M), and Endel ($22M) are general-purpose but lean into ADHD marketing. This is where VC money flows because TAM is larger.

4. **Body doubling is its own thriving vertical.** Five funded platforms compete (FLOWN, Flow Club, Focusmate, Deepwrk, Caveday) plus free options (BodyDoubling.space). UK Access to Work funding is a major growth driver for FLOWN and Deepwrk.

5. **Voice capture is fragmented and no ADHD-specific leader exists.** Multiple tools (Voicy, Brain Dump, Audionotes, Willow) serve the voice-first capture need but none has achieved meaningful scale or ADHD-specific traction.

## 4. Gaps: Problems With No ADHD-Specific Tool

Mapping against our problem IDs from S3:

| Problem ID | Problem | Dedicated ADHD Tool Exists? | Best Available | Gap Assessment |
|------------|---------|---------------------------|----------------|----------------|
| **FP01** Task Initiation | Partial | Goblin Tools (breakdown), Neurolist (one-at-a-time), body doubling platforms | Multiple tools address task breakdown, but **no tool specifically targets the initiation moment** (the gap between knowing what to do and starting). Body doubling is the closest but is session-based. |
| **FP02** Focus/Distraction | Yes (many) | Brain.fm, Endel, Focus Bear, Freedom, Forest, Opal, Sidekick | **Most crowded category.** Focus audio and distraction blocking are well-served. Remaining gap: **adaptive, context-aware focus support** that responds to real-time attention state. |
| **FP03** Time Blindness | Partial | Tiimo, Weel, Structured, Llama Life | Several visual timer/planner tools exist. Gap: **no tool provides real-time "time elapsed" awareness during tasks** (most only show upcoming schedule, not how long you've been on current task vs. plan). |
| **FP04** Deadline Misses | Partial | Motion, Reclaim (auto-scheduling around deadlines) | General tools handle this; **no ADHD-specific tool addresses the "deadline sprint" pattern** where all work happens in final hours. |
| **FP05** Prioritisation | Partial | Fluidwave, Amazing Marvin, Leantime | AI prioritisation exists but **no tool addresses interest-based nervous system** (the ADHD mechanism where urgency/novelty/interest override importance). |
| **FP06** Detail Errors | **NO** | Grammarly (general writing only) | **LARGEST GAP.** No ADHD-specific tool for attention-to-detail errors in workplace deliverables. Grammarly catches writing errors but not data entry errors, spreadsheet mistakes, code review oversights, or process-step omissions. |
| **FP07** Meeting Memory | **NO** (ADHD-specific) | Otter.ai, Fireflies, Granola (all general) | General meeting transcription is mature ($100M+ ARR at Otter, $1B at Fireflies) but **zero ADHD-specific meeting tools** exist. No tool addresses ADHD-specific problems like zoning out mid-meeting, failing to capture action items assigned to you, or forgetting verbal commitments. Recallify and Caption.Ed market to ADHD but are early-stage. |
| **FP08** Project Neglect | Partial | Leantime (Techstars '23, tiny) | **Significant gap.** Leantime is the only ADHD-specific PM tool and has $36K revenue. No tool addresses delay discounting or motivational decay in long-term projects. |
| **FP09** Email Management | **NO** (ADHD-specific) | Saner.AI (early), SaneBox/Boomerang (general) | **Major gap.** No ADHD-specific email tool addresses the prospective memory failure pattern (reading email, intending to respond, then forgetting). Saner.AI is attempting this but is pre-seed with mixed reviews. |
| **FP11** Documentation | **NO** | Goblin Tools (task breakdown), Voicy (dictation) | **Major gap.** No ADHD-specific tool for documentation paralysis -- the inability to write reports, update documentation, or complete written deliverables. Voice-first capture tools partially help but don't solve the synthesis/composition problem. |
| **FP12** Chronic Lateness | Partial | Focus Bear ("Late No More"), Tiimo, Brili | A few tools address this but **no dedicated ADHD lateness tool** with multi-alarm, preparation checklists, and travel time awareness combined. Weel Planner's multi-alarm feature is closest. |

### Summary of Gap Severity

| Gap Level | Problems | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **No ADHD tool at all** | FP06 (Detail Errors), FP07 (Meeting Memory), FP09 (Email), FP11 (Documentation) | These four problems have zero dedicated ADHD solutions. General tools exist but don't address ADHD-specific mechanisms. |
| **ADHD tools exist but tiny/emerging** | FP01 (Initiation), FP05 (Prioritisation), FP08 (Project Neglect), FP12 (Lateness) | Tools exist but are indie-scale (<$250K revenue, <50K users). No clear market leader. |
| **Well-served** | FP02 (Focus), FP03 (Time Blindness), FP04 (Deadlines) | Multiple tools with meaningful traction. Focus audio and visual planners are the most mature ADHD-specific categories. |
